Origin of the Cherry Blossom

The Japanese Cherry Blossom The Japanese cherry blossom (sakura) originated from wild species in Asia, likely the Himalayas and China, before being brought to Japan. While early cultivation has roots in China, the iconic culture of hanami (flower viewing) and the development of hundreds of ornamental varieties, notably the Somei-yoshino, flourished in Japan over centuries, starting in the Heian period.

Cherry Blossom festivals

Japan's cherry blossom festivals, or hanami (flower viewing), generally occur from late March to early April in major cities like Tokyo and Kyoto, with the season starting in Okinawa in January and ending in Hokkaido in May
